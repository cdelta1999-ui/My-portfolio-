The story behind the data

They had the answers — just not the why.

The HR department at Salifort Motors had a problem they couldn't see clearly. Good people were walking out the door, so HR surveyed their workforce and gathered data on thousands of employees — satisfaction, project loads, hours, tenure, promotions, salary bands.

And then they got stuck. The data sat in a spreadsheet, full of answers but speaking a language no one could read. They had the what, but not the why. So they came to me with a single, very human question:

"What's likely to make an employee leave the company?"

My job was two-fold: understand the real drivers of attrition, and build a model that predicts — before it happens — whether an employee is likely to quit. Spot a flight risk early, and you can act early. Keeping a great employee who was about to leave is one of the highest-return moves an HR team can make.

Executive summary

A champion model HR can act on.

Salifort loses roughly 1 in 6 employees, and replacing each is expensive. Using ~12,000 employee records, a tuned Random Forest flags at-risk employees with high accuracy — letting HR shift from reactive replacement to proactive retention.

Key drivers of turnover

Four distinct clusters of risk.

01

Workload extremes

Project count + monthly hours is the single strongest predictor — splitting into burnout (6–7 projects, 175+ hrs/mo) and boreout (under-used on only 2 projects).

02

The 5-year itch

Attrition risk peaks sharply as employees approach their 4th and 5th years — a predictable window for intervention.

03

The pay gap

Staff in the "low" salary band leave at roughly 4× the rate of other bands — a clear, addressable driver.

04

Unrewarded excellence

High evaluation scores often correlate with higher departure risk — a signal of missing mobility or recognition.

Interactive dashboard

A four-step decision journey.

The findings ship as a guided flow — Overview → Why They Leave → Predictive Model → Profiles & Actions — so a non-technical HR lead moves from the big picture to a specific action in minutes.

Strategic HR recommendations

From prediction to action.

  • 1

    Cap workloads

    Trigger an HR review for anyone on 6+ projects or logging over 240 hours/month.

  • 2

    Re-engage the under-used

    Identify low-satisfaction employees with little project exposure and assign meaningful work.

  • 3

    Run proactive 4-year reviews

    Schedule targeted pay, promotion, and growth conversations between years 4 and 5 — before exit interviews.

  • 4

    Decouple recognition from hours

    Reward business impact, not raw volume of hours worked.

  • 5

    Deploy the model responsibly

    Predictions drive supportive outreach only, with full transparency.

Ethics first: the model is a tool for support, not surveillance — every prediction triggers a constructive, human-led conversation, never a penalty.
